
# Happiness vs. The Dream: my Perspective

Many people think that money buys happiness. However, I believe they might be confusing happiness with "The Dream," or perhaps they don't truly know what happiness is in the first place.

## 🍕 What Happiness Means to Me
For me, happiness lives in the simple, everyday things achieved with an average, stable salary:
* **Connections:** Having a girlfriend, spending time with family, and hanging out with friends.
* **Simplicity:** Owning a modest apartment and eating pizza on a Sunday evening.
* **Freedom:** Going to the beach (which is free), swimming, and collecting things.

Does money matter? **Yes, it does.** But money isn't happiness itself; it is simply the **tool** that enables it.

---

## 🇺🇸 What "The Dream" Means to Me
Then, there is "The Dream."

My dream is to become financially independent and build a successful, revenue-generating company in the USA.

I don't want this wealth to show off in luxury restaurants. I want it for two specific reasons:
1. To secure **financial freedom**.
2. To guarantee a stable and safe future for my future family.

---

## 🧠 Conclusion
I just described my dream. Yet, if someone were to ask me right now to define exactly what happiness is in a single sentence... I still wouldn't know how to answer.

Maybe happiness isn't a final destination, but the ability to enjoy the simple moments today while building a bigger dream for tomorrow.
